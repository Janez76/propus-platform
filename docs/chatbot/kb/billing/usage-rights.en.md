# Image Usage Rights

When you order a shoot from Propus, you should know what you may do with it.

## Standard usage rights

On payment of the invoice, the customer receives:

- **Unlimited usage rights** to the delivered images, videos, Matterport
  tours and floor plans
- **No geographic restrictions** — including international marketing
- **For the property named in the order and its marketing context**

In plain terms: you may use the images on your website, in listings, in
print, on social media, and in property presentations for as long as you
like.

## What the usage rights do **not** cover

- **Reselling** images to third parties (stock agencies, image marketplaces)
- **Using them for properties other than the one named in the order**
- **Use in third-party advertising** unconnected to the depicted property
- **Stripping the credit** where it is editorially required (specialist
  publications)

## When owner and broker differ

Common case: broker books and pays → receives the usage rights. If the
property is handed off (mandate ends), usage rights between broker and
owner should be clarified between them. Propus does not mediate in such
follow-on uses.

If the owner needs independent usage rights, they can license the shots
separately from us — reach out.

## Images containing people

People are not depicted in marketing shoots as a rule. If passers-by
appear unintentionally in exterior shots, we retouch or pixelate them by
default.

## Copyright

Copyright remains with the photographer — Swiss standard, not negotiable.
Practical impact for the customer: none, as long as they stay within
standard usage rights. For edge cases, write to office@propus.ch.
